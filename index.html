Keys to Napoleon's Success

Organization

He created the concept of Corps with the Military, whereby self contained combined arms of infantry, cavalry, and artillery could maneuver and fight independently of the army as a whole. If any corps became engaged by enemy fire, it would fight a defensive action while the other Corps concentrated retailiation. His use of a General Staff and a Chief of Staff was also revolutionary for that time period.

Logistical Structure

Napoleon's structures for logistics and planning were unparalled. His prowess in fields relating to hierarchy, social structure, nobility, etc. worked successfully within his armies for decades. A new structure based mainly on merit, was refreshing for the people of France, it was a strucutre they hadn't been accustom to.

Popularity

Napeoloen was very popular among his men, they trusted him almost completely, and with everything they owned or held dear. They would follow their fearless leader into battle everyday, and as such, their leader needed to be both charismatic and cunning. Napoleon was both and much more. Deified by his armies, Napoleon became what others could only dream of.

Strategy

Strategy was a large part of Napoleon's success, with some historian's saying that he was the first notable user of the Blitzkrieg (a popular strategy of war during WW2). His tactics were overtly aggressive but also included misdirection and aspects of a "divide and conquer" tactic. His positioning and military prowess utilized in Austerlitz are one of the many examples why Napoleon is considered one of the greatest Military minds to date.

Personality

It's accurate to say that Napoleon was loved by the majority of the nation, after all, he did come in and establish a semi-stable government. His charismatic and authoritative leadership style made it easy for his subjects to like him, paired with his talent on the battlefield, it's simple to see why the country was raving. People trusted Napoleon, for whatever reason, they found him to be a friendly-face, one they could identify with. These personality traits were crucial to his success in becoming Emperor of France.

Use of Religion

The Catholic Church, a behemoth of people and power, single handedly changing the course of history multiple times throughout the centuries. Napoleon realized the potential power gained by presiding in an administrative fashion over such a giant, his careful planning and cunning combined, provided a new form of power. Religious power, one of the greatest achievements Napoleon made, an essential move to securing the religious within the Country. Establishing himself akin to a diety, Napoleon undoubtedly did this for personal gains and not religious piety. An important distinction to make.

Cursory Look at Napoleon Bonaparte's Military Rise

Summary

Napoleon was born on the island of Corsica in 1769. He was born to a family that had a French nobility status, but only recently. Young Napoleon was sent off to the Royal Military School in 1777 to study the military life. He continued through various military schools until graduating as a Second Lieutenant in the artillery. Napoleon played in a significant part in the French Revolution. his military education helped him fight tremendously well, even defeating the British at Toulon. As a result of that battle, Napoleon was made Brigadier General, after some time, Napoleon married Josephine de Beauharnais. Around this time, Napoleon had controlled the French Army located within Italy, where he later defeated the Austrians in 1797.


The victory advanced Napoleon’s standing in France, and when he returned, he was greeted by cheers and overwhelming popularity. Ready to get back in the fight, Napoleon was directed to take an army on a campaign in Egypt. The objective was to occupy Egypt and sabotage British trade to India. The campaign was carried out with many difficulties and did not going at all how Napoleon had hoped, in addition, back in France, the Directory was losing power. Napoleon took this as an opportunity to advance his political standing, and abandoned his army to return to Paris and become the first consul within France’s new government.


With this new political power, Napoleon instituted additional sanctions in order to retain the power he had just obtained. Napoleon realized that the church was a valuable asset in his pursuit for power, so in 1801, Napoleon successfully ended the divide between France and Church.
